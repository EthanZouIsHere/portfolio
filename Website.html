Plants: Introduction

Images Plants

There are over 390,000 different species of plants all over the world. Plants give humans the oxygen they need by using a process called photosynthesis. During photosynthesis, plants use their leaves to trap light energy and they use the sun's energy to change water and carbon dioxide into glucose.

Vascular and Non-Vascular Plants

There are two main types of plants: Vascular and Non-Vascular Plants. Vascular plants have lignified tissues to conduct water. Some examples are flowers, grasses, and vines. Vascular plants have a shoot system, a root system, and a vascular system.
